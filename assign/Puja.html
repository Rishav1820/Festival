Durga Puja, which is often refered to as Durgotsov is the main festival of Hindus. It is massively celebrated in the states of Bengal, Orrisa, Assam as well as Bangladesh.

The history of this massively celebrated festival dates back to very old times, and there have been various sitings about who celebrated it first. But moving aside, Devi Durga is known to be a mythical deity in accordance to Hindu scriptures, who gets bestowed with charecteristic weapons from various gods to kill the devil Mahisashur. It is basically a significance of removing all the evils and bringing in purity amongst all living creatures and it epitomises the victory of good over evil.

The festival is observed in the Indian calendar month of Ashwin, which corresponds to the months of Septemberâ€“October in the Gregorian calendar,and is a ten-day festival,of which the last five are of significance. Durga puja is believed to commemorate Devi Durga's visit to her natal home along with her children Lakshmi, Swaraswati, Kartikeya and Ganesha. The festival marks its begining from Mahalaya, a day which marks the advent of Devi Durga from her marital home in Kailash. The next significant day is the sixt day, i.e MahaSashti, on which devotees welcome the goddess and her children and the festive celebrations are inaugarated. The seventh(MahaSaptami), eighth(MahaAshthami), nineth(MahaNabami) mark the main days of worship with recitation of scriptures and pujas. The tenth(Dashami) marks the end of the festival as the sculptures get immersed in the water(refered to as Pratima Bisorjon) by the devotees as she descends towards her marital home after the 5day stay at her natal.

During this 5days and prior to that, the entire city of Kolkata lights up to celebrate their indigenous festival. Pandals start getting build right by the end of Mahalaya or even before. Renowned places as well as ancient families get to creative designing of the idols as well as pandals which later on becomes a must visit for all the Kolkata people when they come out for pandal hopping. Families or with friends, its always a rush at the doorsteps of big renowned pandals all throughout the 5days. The streets get filled with food stalls for pandal hoppers. In Bengalis, its more of a custom to spend one of these days on late night pandal hopping.

It turns into 5day long strechted holiday for everyone and they enjoy the days to its fullest. The festival brings in a break from the monotonous hectic life cycle of people as well as it marks the victory of GOOD over the EVIL.
